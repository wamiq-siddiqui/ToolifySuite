    Why compress images?

    Large images slow down websites, waste bandwidth and take longer to share or upload. By compressing images before using them on a website, blog, store or social media, you reduce loading time without changing the visible quality for most viewers.

    This tool focuses on practical, everyday optimization: good balance between quality and file size, support for popular formats and a clean, distraction-free interface.

    Which image formats are supported?

    You can upload common formats such as JPG, PNG and WebP. The output can stay in the original format or be converted to JPG, PNG or WebP depending on your needs.

    Does the compression run online or offline?

    All compression and resizing happens directly in your browser using HTML5 canvas APIs. Your files are not uploaded to any remote server, which keeps the process private and fast.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Is this image compressor safe to use?

    Yes. The entire process runs inside your browser. Images are not uploaded to any external server, and nothing is stored online.

    Will the image quality look bad after compression?

    With the default balanced quality, most images keep their visual clarity while becoming much smaller in size. You can increase or decrease the quality using the slider.

    What does “Remove EXIF / metadata” do?

    Many photos contain extra information such as camera model, location and creation date. Removing metadata reduces size and protects privacy.

    Can I resize and compress at the same time?

    Yes. You can enable the resize option, set a target width or height and keep the aspect ratio checked. The tool will resize and compress in a single step.

    How many images can I compress at once?

    The tool is optimized for up to 20 images at a time, with a recommended maximum size of around 15 MB per image to keep performance smooth.

    Do you keep any copies of my images?

    No. Everything is processed in-memory in your browser tab. Once you close or refresh the page, all images and compressed results are gone.
